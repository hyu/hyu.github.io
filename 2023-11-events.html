Slightly Positive Ambivalent

  • Pageant - Tess Dworman - “emerges from a satirical questioning into the consumption of experimental performance and the ways in which capitalism provokes a savior complex in artists, urging us to demonstrate goodness amidst crises.” Huh, she does Voiceover
  • Brick Theater - Faust - “When you’re old and you can’t focus and you can’t have it all, maybe you can make a deal with the devil — if you’re special. Inspired by failure, Eric Dyer of Radiohole performs a manic version of the Faust legend, inspired by Goethe, F.W. Mernau, Jan Švankmaje, Joe Frank (and so on and so forth).” “In lieu of conjuring the dark forces of the underworld, Dyer metastasizes a participatory meta-fiction, full of apology, goofs, and stutter all wrapped up in a never not-charming DIY aesthetic.” (Review)
